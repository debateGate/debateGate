A good discussion on the internet is a rare thing; "debates" in comment sections are closer to flame wars than discourse, mostly because of the lack of one of two things:

  1. Earnest interest in engaging in debate (e.g. trolling)
  2. Lack of knowledge about what a good argument is and how to create one in a debate.
Ideally, voting would rectify the first issue just like it usually does on Reddit or Hacker News, and the design of debateGate attempts to curb the second issue as much as possible by simplifying the debate process.

An argument is, when all the stylistic and conversational aspects are stripped away, a list of little lingustic constructs called enthymemes. An enthymeme is made up of two parts: the first is called a thesis, and the second is called the justification.

An example of a thesis is this:

We should make ice cream free for all, because ice cream improves mental health.

The first part, "We should make ice cream free," is the thesis. It is the thing that you believe -- the policy decision that you would like to see enacted. Theses come in all types, from "Billy is a moron" to "we should subsidize corn." Regardless of the silliness of the thesis, they all have justifications of some type. The support, in this case, is the part of the enthymeme that is found after the "because": "ice cream improves mental health."

The reasoning in the justification must justify the cost of implementing/believing in the thesis. For instance, "deport all the poor" is not a good way to get rid of homelessness, even if deporting all the poor would technically do so, because the harmful effects of doing so outweigh the benefits.

A good way to test if your enthymeme makes sense is to reverse the justification and the thesis. The above claim would become: "Because ice cream improves general mental health, we should make ice cream free." Sometimes you will be able so find mistakes or logical fallacies in your enthymeme this way.

debateGate strips debate down to its bare minimum to enforce proper argument on people, so these rules are a great way to get started winning debates! Mastering the enthymeme and the rules that go along with the enthymeme will not only improve your debateGating, but will also help you in real life and elsewhere on the internet.
